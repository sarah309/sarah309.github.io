Content

Home page

This site is geared toward college students that feel overwhelmed from classes, assignments, jobs, extracurriculars, social obligations, and the many other accruing responsibilities that come from living on their own. Because of the difficulties students face with finding time to prepare healthy meals, fast food and processed snacks often become the norm from day to day. This website offers students quick, affordable, and nutritious recipes so they can enjoy balanced meals on a college schedule.

Recipes

Breakfast Overnight Oats: Ingredients Rolled oats Fruit of choice (I suggest frozen mixed berries) Coconut water Add oats and fruit to bowl, and enough water to barely cover. Stir. Let sit in fridge overnight. Enjoy the next morning! Lunch Veggie Sandwich: Ingredients Bread Any veggies/condiments you’d like Recommendations: Cucumber Tomato Mushrooms Onion Lettuce Dill pickles Mustard dressing Hummus Instructions Assemble your sandwich with the veggies and condiments of your choice and enjoy! Dinner Curry: Ingredients 2 cloves garlic (or 1 Tbsp garlic powder) 1/2 large onion (or 3 Tbsp dried minced onion) 3-4 large potatoes 1 tbsp curry powder 1 tsp garam masala (optional) 1 tsp turmeric 1 can light coconut milk 1 tbsp tomato paste 5 oz fresh or canned tomatoes 1/2 - 1 C peas (I use frozen) Large handfuls of spinach Brown rice (as much as you want) Instructions Dice potatoes into large chunks (no need to peel) and add to a pot with enough water to cover. Bring to a boil and then reduce heat to lowest setting. In a separate pot, add rice (1 C dried brown rice produces about 2 1/2 C cooked rice) with enough water to fill the pot (about 6 C water per 1 C rice). Add chopped onion and minced garlic to rice (if you’re using dried, wait until later step before adding). The rice will be cooked in a similar way that you’d cook pasta. Bring rice to a boil and then reduce heat to lowest setting. With the burners on medium-low, if you let the rice and potatoes sit for an hour they should be good to go for the next step. A fork should easily go through the potatoes, and you can taste test the rice to see if it’s been cooked to your preference. Drain the rice (I just use the pot lid, open it just a crack and pour out the water.) Put the pot back on the stovetop (with burner off). Pour most (about 3/4) of the potato water out. Return to stovetop on medium-low setting. Mash the potatoes until your preferred consistency (I enjoy mine a little chunky). Add garlic powder and onion powder (if using) to potatoes. Stir. Add coconut milk, tomato paste, diced tomatoes, and spinach. Stir. Once it starts simmering, add remaining spices. Add the frozen peas as late as possible (they lose their bright green color if cooked for long). Stir. Enjoy your potato curry over the brown rice.
